Continious optimisation

Continious optimisation studies how to find the minima (lowest point) of a smooth function under smooth constraints. In the unconstrainted case our objective then is \[ \min_{x \in \mathbb{R}^{n}} f(x) \] where $f: \mathbb{R} ^{n} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ is continious. In the constrained case we typically apply an equality, inequality or perhaps geometric constraint (or some combination of these). Points that satisfy these inequalities are called `feasible' points. \[ \min_{x \in \Omega} f(x) \\ \text{subject to:} \\ c_i(x) = 0 \; \forall \; i \in E \\ c_i(x) \geq 0 \; \forall \; i \in I \] where $f: \mathbb{R}^{n} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ is continious, $c_i: \mathbb{R} ^{n} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ for all $i \in E \cup I$ are continious and $\Omega$ is a closed convex subset of $\mathbb{R}^n$. Here $E$ is the equality constraints, $I$ the set of inequality constraints and $\Omega$ represents a geometric constraint. Notes coming soon!
